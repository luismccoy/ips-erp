## Photography Strategy for IPS-ERP

### Where to Source Professional Medical Photography

#### Stock Photo Sources (High Quality):
1. **Unsplash** (free, high quality)
   - Search: "nurse home care", "elderly patient nurse", "healthcare professional"
   - Quality level: Professional but may be overused

2. **iStock / Getty Images** (paid, exclusive)
   - Medical/Healthcare category
   - Latin American models available
   - Rights-managed for exclusivity
   - Cost: $50-500 per image

3. **Pexels** (free)
   - "Home healthcare" category
   - Growing medical collection
   - Free for commercial use

4. **Shutterstock Healthcare Collection** (paid)
   - Extensive medical library
   - Filter by ethnicity/region
   - Cost: Subscription $29-199/month

5. **Adobe Stock** (paid)
   - Premium quality
   - Advanced filtering
   - Integration with design tools

#### What to Look For:
- **Real medical scenarios** (not staged corporate shots)
- **Natural lighting** (not overly polished studio shots)
- **Cultural relevance** (Latin American healthcare professionals when possible)
- **Dignity and respect** (elderly patients treated with respect, not condescension)
- **Authentic interactions** (nurse-patient connection, not just poses)

#### Specific Image Types Needed:

**Hero Section:**
- Nurse with elderly patient in home setting (warm, natural light)
- Healthcare professional reviewing tablet/clipboard
- Home visit scenario (living room, bedroom backdrop)

**Trust/About Section:**
- Team of healthcare professionals (diverse, professional attire)
- Medical certifications/compliance imagery
- Technology in use (not just screenshots)

**Features Section:**
- Nurse using mobile device
- Admin at computer (office setting)
- Family member viewing portal

## Color Psychology for Enterprise Healthcare

### What Works (Research-Backed):

**Primary: Deep Blue (#1e40af, #1e3a8a)**
- Trust, professionalism, stability
- Used by: Axxess, WellSky, most healthcare enterprise
- Psychology: Calming, reduces anxiety, conveys expertise

**Secondary: Teal/Cyan (#14b8a6, #0891b2)**
- Modern healthcare, innovation
- Bridge between blue (trust) and green (health)
- Less common = differentiation

**Accent: Warm Orange (#f97316, #ea580c)**
- Action, energy, urgency (for CTAs)
- Contrast with blue creates attention
- Used sparingly for conversions

**Supporting: Slate/Gray (#475569, #334155)**
- Sophistication, neutrality
- Professional backgrounds
- Text hierarchy

### What to AVOID:
- Bright purple (too consumer, not enterprise)
- Neon colors (unprofessional)
- Pure black backgrounds (harsh, intimidating)
- Pastel everything (looks weak)

## Typography for Trust

### Recommended Font Pairings:

**Option 1: Professional & Modern**
- **Headings**: Inter (Google Fonts, free)
  - Used by major SaaS (Linear, Vercel)
  - Clean, highly legible
  - Weights: 600 (Semibold) for headlines
  
- **Body**: Inter (same family for consistency)
  - Weight: 400 (Regular) for body
  - Size: 16px minimum
  - Line height: 1.6 (readable)

**Option 2: Corporate Authority**
- **Headings**: Montserrat (Google Fonts, free)
  - Strong, geometric
  - Enterprise feel
  
- **Body**: Open Sans
  - Highly readable
  - Healthcare industry standard

**Option 3: Sophisticated**
- **Headings**: Poppins (Google Fonts, free)
  - Modern but not trendy
  - Good weight variation
  
- **Body**: Roboto
  - Google's design, trusted
  - Excellent screen rendering

### Typography Rules:
- **Minimum body text**: 16px (accessibility)
- **Line height**: 1.5-1.7 for body text
- **Heading scale**: 1.25 ratio (16, 20, 25, 31, 39, 49px)
- **Font weights**: Use 400 (regular) and 600 (semibold) only
- **Letter spacing**: -0.02em for large headings

## Layout Patterns That Convey Professionalism

### 1. Hero Section Structure

**Enterprise Pattern:**
```
[Centered alignment]
- Small trust badge/announcement bar
- Large headline (2 lines max)
- Descriptive subheadline (single line)
- Dual CTAs (Primary + Secondary)
- Stats bar or trust logos below
- Hero image/video (right 50% or full background)
```

**NOT:**
- Left-aligned with weird angles
- Too much text (paragraphs in hero)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Photography > Graphics**: Real humans > illustrations/emojis
2. **Data > Claims**: "92% defended" > "We're the best"
3. **Partnership > Transaction**: Long-term language, not one-off sales
4. **Professional > Trendy**: Timeless aesthetic, not following design fads
5. **Enterprise > Consumer**: Sophisticated, not playful
